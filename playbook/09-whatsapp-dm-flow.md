# WhatsApp Community DM Flow (reply to signed client)

The community post's only job is to earn a reply. This file is what you do
after the reply, because a reply is not a client. The money is made in the DM,
then closed on a call.

**The golden rule:** the DM books the call, the call signs the client. Do not
try to close a build inside WhatsApp.

Booking link (tagged so you can see which bookings came from the groups):
`https://calendly.com/tal-foxamit-seatophomes/30min?utm_source=whatsapp&utm_medium=community`

## 5 principles
1. **Reply fast.** Within minutes if you can. First to respond wins most of these.
2. **One ask at a time.** Every message ends with a single question.
3. **Bound your free work.** Send 2 or 3 sharp findings as a taste, save the full
   mockup for the call. Otherwise you do hours of free work for people who ghost.
4. **Always be moving to a call.** Text qualifies, the call closes.
5. **Never dodge price.** If asked directly, give the soft range and pivot to the
   call. Dodging kills trust.

## Stage 1: First reply

**If they sent their website:**
> Appreciate you reaching out 🙏 Taking a look now. Quick question so the audit is actually useful: what's the one thing you'd most want it to do better, more leads, less manual work, or lower software costs?

**If they just said "hi / interested":**
> Great to connect 🙏 Quick question so I can make this useful: what's the business, and what's the main thing you'd want it to do better, more leads, less manual work, or cutting software costs?

**If they have no website yet:**
> No website yet is totally fine, honestly that's often the bigger opportunity. Tell me a bit about the business and I'll put together a free mockup of what a site could look like for you. What do you do?

## Stage 2: The taste + book the call

After they answer, send 2 or 3 specific things (real findings if they have a
site, ideas if they don't), then pivot straight to the call:

> Got it. A few things already jumping out: (1) [quick finding], (2) [quick finding], (3) [quick finding]. There's more, and I can build you a mockup of a better version. Easiest if I walk you through it live, takes about 15 minutes. What day works this week? Or grab a time here: https://calendly.com/tal-foxamit-seatophomes/30min?utm_source=whatsapp&utm_medium=community

This is the pivot point. You gave value, so asking for 15 minutes feels fair,
not pushy.

## Stage 3: On the call (talking points, not a script)
1. Ask about the business: how they get customers, what eats their time, what
   they pay monthly for software.
2. Show the mockup. Let them react.
3. Recommend the smallest thing that solves their biggest pain. Do not oversell.
4. State the price plainly, then apply the 10%.
5. Get a yes to the next step before hanging up.

## Stage 4: Close (right after the call)

> Great talking 🙏 Quick recap of what we'd do: [2 to 3 bullet scope]. Investment is [$X], and the 10% community discount brings it to [$Y]. To lock it in I take a deposit and we can start [this week]. Want me to send over the agreement?

Send the agreement link from the site so they can sign and pay in one place.

## Stage 5: Follow-up for quiet leads (most of your money is here)

Most people go quiet once, not because they said no. Nudge, don't nag.

- **+1 day:** `Hey [name], did that mockup idea sound useful? Happy to build it, just need a good time for a quick call.`
- **+3 days (add value):** `One more thing I noticed on your site: [one quick idea]. Easy fix. Want me to show you the rest?`
- **+7 days (the breakup):** `I'll stop filling your inbox 🙂 If the timing's ever right, the free audit and mockup offer stands. Wishing you a good week.`

The breakup message often gets the most replies. People respond when they think
the door is closing.

## Objection handling

- **"How much does it cost?"**
  > Depends on scope, but projects start around a few thousand and most land well below what people expect. Fastest way to a real number is a quick 15-min call, want to grab a time?

- **"That's expensive."**
  > Fair. Worth comparing it to what you're already paying: monthly software fees never stop and rise every year, this you own once. And it's built to bring in more work than it costs. I can also split it into payments.

- **"I already have someone / a website."**
  > Totally fair. Want a free second-opinion audit anyway? Worst case you walk away with a few free ideas, no pressure.

- **"Let me think about it."**
  > Of course. Want me to build the free mockup in the meantime, so you've got something concrete to think about instead of a maybe?

- **"Not sure I need this."**
  > No worries, might not. Can I ask, what's the most annoying or time-consuming part of running the business right now? If it's not something I can fix, I'll tell you straight.

## Make it actually run
1. **Use the booking link** in the flow instead of "what day works." It filters
   for serious people and saves the back-and-forth.
2. **Log every reply in the CRM** with the source tagged `whatsapp`, so nothing
   goes cold and you can see which channel is actually producing clients.
3. **Track the funnel:** replies to this post, calls booked, calls held, clients
   signed. After the first batch you will know your real reply-to-client rate and
   where people drop off.
